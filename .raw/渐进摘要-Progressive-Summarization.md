# 渐进摘要 (Progressive Summarization)

## Definition

Progressive Summarization is a layered note-distillation method developed by Tiago Forte. Instead of trying to summarize everything at once, you progressively distill information across repeated passes. This solves the tension between compression (short, glanceable notes) and context (enough detail to understand later).

## The Five Layers

| Layer | Action | When |
|-------|--------|------|
| **1. Capture** | Save raw material (quotes, key passages) | First encounter |
| **2. Bold** | Bold the most important sentences | Days later, on review |
| **3. Highlight** | Highlight only the "best of the best" within bolded text | Weeks later |
| **4. Mini-Summary** | Write 2-3 sentence executive summary in your own words at top | When note is revisited for use |
| **5. Synthesize / Remix** | Connect insights across notes; create new ideas | During active projects |

## Why It Solves the Dense-Note Problem

> [!tip] The user's problem: "Notes become extremely rich from Q&A → harder to retrieve when I forget."

Progressive summarization directly addresses this:
- **Glanceability**: At a glance, you can see how important a note is by how many layers it contains
- **Retrieval speed**: One documented case showed retrieval dropping from **12 minutes to under 30 seconds**
- **No need to re-read everything**: Layers 1-4 let you skip straight to what matters

## Practical Adaptations

- **Compressed single-session approach**: Do Layers 1-3 in one session if you engage intensively then move on (Frank Anaya's adaptation)
- **Don't apply all layers to all notes**: Only ~1 in 10 notes needs all 5 layers. Prioritize notes tied to current learning.
- **Mini-summaries must be your own**: AI summaries feel flat because they lack your personal context. Progressive summarization works because *you* do the filtering.
- **AI can help with mechanical layers**: Let AI bold/highlight/draft summaries, but keep Layer 4-5 human-driven.

## Relationship to This Vault

The user's current practice — creating rich concept pages through Q&A that grow over time — is a form of progressive summarization *in reverse*: the note starts rich and needs to be *retrievable*. The missing step is adding Layers 3-4 (highlight key takeaways; write mini-summary at the top) so the note is glanceable without re-reading the entire thing.

> [!tip] After each Q&A session, add a 2-3 sentence mini-summary at the TOP of the note summarizing what was learned. This is the single highest-ROI change for retrieval.
